Sizzling Sisig

It's too good to share

The origin of Sisig was during the American occupation of the Philippines. During this time the Filipinos who didn't want any food waste chose to marinade and barbecue the parts of the meat that didn't reach the American soldiers and sold it to the locals. The legend of Sisig was born and so has its descendants. To cater to those who don't want to eat the unusual parts of the pig, people have started making it solely with pork belly.

Ingredients:

  • 500 g pork belly, sliced
  • 1 red onion, finely chopped
  • 1-2 chili peppers or 1/4 tbsp. chili flakes
  • 1/2 lemon juice
  • 1/2 tbsp. soy sauce
  • 1/8 ground pepper
  • a pinch of salt

Steps:

  1. Season the pork belly with sal and pepper and marinate for at least 30 minutes
  2. Grill the pork belly until it is well done (the skin should be crispy)
  3. Chop the pork belly into small pieces
  4. Combine the chopped meat with onions, lemon juice, chili, then season with soy sauce and pepper
  5. Get a sizzling plate and add some butter, then add the sisig
